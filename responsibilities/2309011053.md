## 1. Image-as-sequence framing

To compare RNN families against the convolutional models on the *same* dataset, I framed each 28×28 image as a length-28 sequence of 28-dim row vectors. This is the most natural sequential decomposition of an image and matches how PixelRNN-style models scan images.

The encoder runs the chosen recurrent cell over all 28 rows and takes the **final hidden state** as the bottleneck. The decoder repeats that bottleneck 28 times and runs the same cell type forward, projecting each output step through a `Linear(hidden → 28)` followed by `Sigmoid`. This produces a 28×28 image, which lets us compute MSE against the original — the *same* loss as Person 3's Conv-AE.

I implemented this with one class — `RNNAutoencoder(cell=…)` — that takes `'rnn' | 'lstm' | 'gru'` and an optional `bidirectional=True` flag, so the four variants share *identical* training code. This is essential for a fair comparison.

## 2. Vanilla RNN, LSTM, GRU — what each cell adds

### Vanilla RNN
$$h_t = \tanh(W_h h_{t-1} + W_x x_t + b)$$

A single `tanh` chain. The gradient of `h_T` w.r.t. `h_1` involves a product of `T` Jacobians, each contracting through `tanh' ≤ 1`. After 28 steps the early-step gradients are vanishingly small — the same vanishing-gradient story we already documented on sigmoid MLPs in Project 1, only applied to time instead of depth.

### LSTM (Hochreiter & Schmidhuber, 1997)
Adds **input / forget / output** gates and a separate **cell state** `c_t` whose update is *additive* not multiplicative — `c_t = f_t · c_{t-1} + i_t · ĉ_t`. The forget gate gives the network direct control over how long information persists, which is precisely what the vanilla RNN cannot do.

### GRU (Cho et al., 2014)
A streamlined LSTM with only **reset** and **update** gates and a single hidden state. ~25% fewer parameters than LSTM at the same hidden size; on short sequences it usually matches LSTM exactly.

### Bidirectional LSTM
Two LSTMs — one left-to-right, one right-to-left — whose hidden states are concatenated. The encoder thereby sees both top-down and bottom-up row context before compressing. I left the *decoder* unidirectional because true bidirectional decoding is non-causal and not standard for autoencoding.

## 3. Empirical results

On 5 epochs of Fashion-MNIST (`15_rnn_loss.png`):

- **Vanilla RNN-AE**: highest validation MSE → visibly blurry reconstructions in `16_rnn_reconstructions.png`. The vanishing-gradient story is empirically reproduced.
- **LSTM-AE / GRU-AE**: substantial drop in MSE — gating works.
- **BiLSTM-AE**: marginal additional improvement — bidirectional encoding helps a small amount, capped by the unidirectional decoder.

All four RNN-AEs are visibly **blurrier** than Person 3's Conv-AE. This isn't a failure of the implementation — it's a direct lesson in inductive bias. RNNs have temporal priors; convolutions have spatial priors; image data has spatial structure. Use the right tool.

## 4. Bonus — Tiny Diffusion Model

I included a deliberately minimal DDPM (Ho, Jain & Abbeel, 2020) implementation to satisfy the instructor's bonus-points policy *("topics not yet covered, provided syllabus topics are thoroughly addressed first")*.

### What I implemented

- **Forward (noising) process** — `q(x_t | x_0) = N(√ᾱ_t · x_0, (1-ᾱ_t) · I)` with a linear β schedule from `1e-4` to `0.02` over `T = 200` steps.
- **Tiny UNet (~80k params)** — single down/up block with sinusoidal time embedding added to the input feature map. Far smaller than a real DDPM UNet; this is a methodological demo.
- **ε-prediction training objective** — `L = E[‖ε − ε_θ(x_t, t)‖²]`. This is the simplified loss from the DDPM paper.
- **Reverse (sampling) process** — iterative denoise from `x_T ~ N(0, I)` back to `x_0` with the standard reverse mean and small added noise except at the final step.

Even with three epochs and 80k parameters, the samples (`17_diffusion_samples.png`) are **recognisable Fashion-MNIST clothing items**. That's the punchline — diffusion's inductive bias (iterative denoising of a fixed noise schedule) is so well-matched to image data that even a toy model produces something usable.

### Conceptual bridge

A diffusion model is, in a precise sense, **a denoising autoencoder run iteratively across many noise levels**. Person 3 trained a single-σ DAE; I trained a continuous-σ DAE conditioned on the noise level. The bonus section is therefore *not* an unrelated tack-on — it's the natural ladder-rung above DAE → VAE → DCGAN.

## 6. Final conclusions

I distilled six findings into REPORT.md §11, plus a one-line design-recommendation table. The headline conclusion:

> **Architecture matters most when matched to the data.** Convolutional models dominate Fashion-MNIST classification *and* generation because their spatial inductive biases match the data. Recurrent models still work — but at a quality cost that is itself the most important methodological lesson of the project.
